What is SwiftBudget?
A quick overview of the system

SwiftBudget is a personal finance manager that helps you track income and expenses, set budgets, monitor spending trends, and stay on top of your financial health.

Key features:

  • Dashboard — Visual overview of your finances with charts and actionable insights
  • Transactions — Record every income and expense with categories and descriptions
  • Budgets — Set spending limits per category and get alerts when you're close
  • Recurring — Automate regular transactions like rent, salary, or subscriptions
  • Projects — Group transactions under specific projects or goals
Getting started? Add a few categories first, then start recording your transactions. The dashboard will populate automatically.
Dashboard
Understanding your financial overview

The dashboard gives you a real-time snapshot of your financial health. Here's what each section means:

Stat Cards (top row)
  • Savings Rate — The percentage of income you're keeping. 20%+ is healthy, 0-19% is low, and negative means you're spending more than you earn.
  • Income — Total income for the selected time range.
  • Balance — Income minus expenses. Red means you're in deficit.
  • Projected — Estimated total expenses by month-end based on your current daily spending pace.
Projected Expense — How It Works

This estimates where your spending will land by month-end:

Projected = (Total Spent So Far ÷ Days Elapsed) × Days in Month

For example, if you've spent MK 50,000 by Day 5 of a 31-day month:
Daily average = MK 10,000 → Projected = MK 310,000

Pulse Chart
  • Green area — Your income over time
  • Red line — Your expenses over time
  • Dashed vertical "Today" line — Marks the current day
  • Dashed red line (after Today) — Projected daily expense if you continue at the same pace
  • Tooltip — Hover any point to see the amount and the change from the previous period
Category Donut

Shows how your expenses break down by category. The total in the center is your overall expenses. Categories under 5% of total are grouped into "Miscellaneous" to keep the chart clean.

Budget vs. Actual
  • Blue bar — Under 80% of budget (on track)
  • Amber bar — 80–99% of budget (caution)
  • Red bar — Over 100% of budget (over-spent)
  • Dark pacer dot — Where you should be based on the current day of the month. If the bar extends past this dot, you're spending faster than your monthly pace.
Time Range

Use the buttons in the top-right (30d, 90d, 6mo, 1yr) to change the reporting period. The chart and all stats update accordingly.

Transactions
Recording and managing your income & expenses

Transactions are the core of SwiftBudget. Every income or expense you record feeds into your dashboard, budgets, and reports.

Adding a Transaction
1
Click "+ New" from the dashboard or "Add Transaction" from the Transactions page.
2
Choose the type: Income or Expense.
3
Select a category (e.g., Food, Transport, Salary).
4
Enter the amount, date, and optional description.
5
Optionally assign it to a project.
6
Click Save. It immediately appears in your dashboard and reports.
Filtering & Searching

Use the search bar at the top to find transactions by description or category name. Use the filters to narrow by date range, type, or category.

Exporting

Click the CSV Export button to download all your filtered transactions as a spreadsheet. The file opens directly in Excel.

Tip: Add transactions as they happen — it's much easier than trying to remember at the end of the week.
Budgets
Setting spending limits and tracking progress

Budgets let you set maximum spending limits per category, so you know when you're approaching or exceeding your targets.

Creating a Budget
1
Go to Budgets from the navigation bar.
2
Click "+ New Budget".
3
Select a category (e.g., Food, Transport).
4
Enter the budget amount — the max you want to spend.
5
Choose the period (monthly is most common).
6
Set an alert threshold (default 80%) — you'll get a dashboard warning when spending reaches this level.
Reading the Progress Bars

On the dashboard, each budget shows a color-coded progress bar:

  • Blue — Under 80%, you're on track
  • Amber — 80–99%, be careful
  • Red — Over 100%, you've exceeded the budget

The small dark dot on each bar shows where you should be based on the current day in the month. If the bar is past the dot, you're spending faster than pace.

Tip: Start with your biggest expense categories (food, rent, transport). You can always add more later.
Recurring Transactions
Automating regular income and expenses

Recurring transactions save you from manually entering the same items each period. Set them up once and they generate automatically.

Setting Up a Recurring Transaction
1
Go to Recurring from the navigation bar.
2
Click "+ New Recurring".
3
Fill in: Amount, Type (income/expense), Category, Frequency (daily/weekly/monthly/yearly).
4
Set the Next Due Date — when the first occurrence should happen.
5
Optionally set an End Date to stop after a certain date.
Processing Due Transactions

Recurring items don't create transactions automatically on page load. You need to click the "Process Due" button. This:

  • Checks all active recurring items whose due date has passed
  • Creates actual transactions for each one
  • Advances the due date to the next period (e.g., +1 month)
Managing Recurring Items
  • Pause/Resume / — Temporarily stop a recurring item without deleting it
  • Edit — Change amount, frequency, category, etc.
  • Delete — Permanently remove the rule (transactions already generated remain)
Tip: Set up recurring items for rent, salary, subscriptions, and utility bills. Click "Process Due" at the start of each month.
Projects
Grouping transactions by goal or event

Projects let you tag transactions with a specific goal or event, so you can track how much a particular effort costs or earns.

Use Cases
  • Home renovation — Track all expenses related to a building project
  • Holiday trip — See exactly what a vacation cost
  • Side hustle — Separate business income and expenses from personal
  • Wedding — Monitor costs across multiple categories
How to Use
1
Go to Projects and click "+ New Project".
2
Give it a name and optional description.
3
When adding or editing a transaction, select this project from the Project dropdown.
4
View the project page to see all associated transactions and totals.
Tips & Best Practices
Get the most out of SwiftBudget
Record Daily

Log transactions as they happen. Waiting until the end of the week leads to forgotten expenses and inaccurate data.

Set Realistic Budgets

Start by tracking for one month without budgets. Then set budgets based on your actual spending patterns, not wishful thinking.

Use the Pacer

The budget pacer dot shows where you should be. If your bar is past the dot mid-month, you need to slow down spending in that category.

Aim for 20% Savings

A savings rate of 20% or higher is considered healthy. The Savings Rate indicator on the dashboard makes this easy to track.

Automate the Obvious

Set up recurring transactions for salary, rent, and subscriptions. Click "Process Due" at the start of each month.

Review Weekly

Spend 5 minutes each Sunday checking your dashboard. Are you on pace? Any budget alerts? This small habit makes a big difference.
